## Strategy: Ornstein-Uhlenbeck (OU) Pairs Trading on S&P 500

### 1. Universe
All S&P 500 constituents (~500 US equities). Daily OHLCV data, 2010-01-01 to 2023-01-01.

### 2. Pair Selection (training window: 2018–2020)
Compute Mean Squared Distance (MSD) for all candidate pairs:
    d_ij = (1/N) * sum((R_i,t - R_j,t)^2)
where R_i,t is the daily return of stock i at time t.
Select pairs with the smallest d_ij; enforce one-to-one mapping (each stock in at most one pair).
Alternative filter: Engle-Granger cointegration test (OLS residuals, p-value < 0.05).

### 3. Spread and OU Model
Spread: S_ij,t = P_i,t - P_j,t  (price difference of closing prices)
Fit OU process: dS_t = lambda*(mu - S_t)*dt + sigma*dW_t
Discretized AR(1): S_{t+1} = a*S_t + b + eps_t
Parameters recovered as:
    lambda = -ln(a) / delta,  mu = b / (1-a),
    sigma  = std(eps) * sqrt(-2*ln(a) / (delta*(1-a^2)))
where delta = 1 (daily sampling).

### 4. Signal Generation (rolling, point-in-time)
Z-score: Z_t = (S_t - mu) / sigma
Map Z_t to empirical percentile using a rolling 90-day window (no future data).
Entry:  short spread when percentile > 75%; long spread when percentile < 25%.
Exit:   when percentile crosses 50% (mean reversion complete).

### 5. Execution
Daily rebalancing; positions entered the next trading day after signal.
No transaction costs. Full position on the spread (long one leg, short the other).
Out-of-sample test: post-2020. OU parameters estimated on 2018–2020 training window only.
