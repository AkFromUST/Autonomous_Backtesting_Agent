## Strategy Logic  

1. **Pair selection (training window 2018‑2020):**  
   - Compute the mean‑squared distance (MSD) of daily returns for every possible pair:  
     \[
     d_{ij}= \frac{1}{N}\sum_{t=1}^{N}(R_{i,t}-R_{j,t})^{2}
     \]
   - Rank pairs by smallest \(d_{ij}\); enforce a one‑to‑one mapping (each stock appears in at most one pair).  
   - Optional filter: retain only pairs that pass an Engle‑Granger cointegration test (p‑value < 0.05).  

2. **Spread definition:**  
   \[
   S_{ij,t}=P_{i,t}-P_{j,t}
   \]
   where \(P_{i,t}\) and \(P_{j,t}\) are daily closing prices.

3. **OU parameter estimation (static, estimated on 2018‑2020 only):**  
   - Fit the discrete AR(1) representation \(S_{t+1}=aS_t+b+\varepsilon_t\).  
   - Recover continuous‑time parameters:  
     \[
     \lambda=-\frac{\ln a}{\Delta},\;
     \mu=\frac{b}{1-a},\;
     \sigma=\text{sd}(\varepsilon_t)\sqrt{\frac{-2\ln a}{\Delta(1-a^{2})}}
     \]
     with \(\Delta=1\) day.

4. **Signal generation (rolling, point‑in‑time):**  
   - Compute the OU z‑score: \(Z_t=\frac{S_t-\mu}{\sigma}\).  
   - Map \(Z_t\) to an empirical percentile using a **90‑day rolling window** that contains only past observations.  
   - **Entry:**  
     - Short spread if percentile > 75 % (high positive z).  
     - Long spread if percentile < 25 % (low negative z).  
   - **Exit:** Close the position when the percentile crosses **50 %** (i.e., z‑score reverts to the median band).  

5. **Rebalancing:** Daily; signals are acted on the next trading day. Full notional exposure is taken on the spread (long one leg, short the other), with no leverage weighting.

## Caveats & Limitations  

- **Zero transaction costs** – the reported Sharpe and returns ignore realistic commissions, bid‑ask spreads, and market impact, which are non‑trivial for a high‑turnover daily strategy.  
- **Static OU parameters** – parameters estimated on 2018‑2020 are held fixed for the entire out‑of‑sample period; any regime shift in mean‑reversion speed (\(\lambda\)) or volatility (\(\sigma\)) is unaccounted for.  
- **One‑to‑one pairing constraint** reduces diversification; the final 45 pairs may be insufficient to average idiosyncratic risk.  
- **Rolling percentile window leakage risk** – although the 90‑day window uses only past data, the percentile calculation can be sensitive to outliers, causing occasional premature exits or entries.  
- **No volatility‑based position sizing** – equal‑notional exposure amplifies risk when a pair’s spread becomes highly volatile.  
- **Sample‑size bias** – selecting pairs with the smallest MSD may over‑fit to noisy co‑movement present only in the training window.
